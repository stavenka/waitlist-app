Arbitrica vs. RoomHawk: Full Comparison

April 1, 2026 · Arbitrica Team

RoomHawk and Arbitrica both help travelers save money on hotel bookings by tracking prices after reservation. Their approaches differ significantly in scope, automation, and the type of savings they uncover.

How RoomHawk Works

RoomHawk monitors over 2 million hotel rooms across major booking platforms. After you make a reservation, RoomHawk tracks the price and sends you an alert when the rate drops. The platform checks prices from a single geographic location and focuses on temporal price changes (rates decreasing due to demand shifts, cancellations, or last-minute inventory adjustments).

RoomHawk charges a percentage of the savings it finds, which aligns incentives but can add up on large bookings. The monitoring is automatic once you set up tracking, and alerts arrive via email.

How Arbitrica Works

Arbitrica tracks both temporal and geographic price changes. The system searches the same hotel from 200 countries simultaneously, catching not only standard price drops but also the much larger geographic pricing differences that hotels use to charge different rates in different markets.

Beyond post-booking monitoring, Arbitrica also compares prices at the point of checkout before you book. The Chrome extension detects hotel checkout pages and runs a 200-country price search in real time. One-click rebooking automates the process of switching to a lower rate.

The Geo-Arbitrage Gap

RoomHawk's 2 million room database is impressive for tracking temporal drops. A hotel that costs $250 today may drop to $220 next week due to lower demand. RoomHawk catches that $30 saving.

Arbitrica finds a different kind of saving entirely. That same $250 hotel room may cost $170 when booked from Indonesia and $185 from Portugal, right now. These geographic differences exist simultaneously and are far larger than typical temporal drops. The average geographic saving is 25-40%, compared to 5-15% for temporal drops.

User Experience Differences

RoomHawk requires manual setup for each booking you want tracked. You enter the hotel details and booking reference, and the system begins monitoring. Alerts arrive via email, and rebooking is manual.

Arbitrica automates detection through its Chrome extension. Bookings are captured automatically when you complete a reservation on any supported platform. When a better price is found, the rebooking process is a single click with validation that the replacement booking matches your original room type and terms.

When RoomHawk Works Well

RoomHawk is a reliable choice for travelers who book domestically and want to catch temporal price drops without the overhead of a Chrome extension. Its large database ensures broad coverage, and the percentage-based pricing means you only pay when you save.

When Arbitrica Is the Better Choice

For international travelers and anyone booking hotels where geographic pricing differences are significant (which is most hotels), Arbitrica delivers substantially larger savings. The 200-country search accesses price tiers that RoomHawk cannot see, and the combined pre-checkout and post-booking coverage means no savings opportunity is missed.
