Arbitrica vs. Pruvo: Which Saves You More?

April 3, 2026 · Arbitrica Team

Pruvo is one of the most established hotel price trackers on the market. It has helped travelers save on post-booking price drops since its launch. Arbitrica takes a fundamentally different approach to the same problem. Here is a direct comparison of the two tools.

How Pruvo Works

Pruvo monitors hotel prices after you book. You forward your booking confirmation email to check@pruvo.com, and Pruvo begins tracking the rate. If the price drops on the same platform, Pruvo sends you an alert. You then manually cancel your old reservation and rebook at the lower rate.

Pruvo is free for basic tracking. The premium tier adds features like priority monitoring and multi-booking management. The system is straightforward and does what it promises.

How Arbitrica Works

Arbitrica operates in two modes. The pre-checkout mode detects when you are about to book a hotel and compares prices across 200 countries before you pay. The post-booking mode monitors your existing reservations for price drops across all 200 regions until check-in. When a lower rate is found, one-click rebooking handles the cancellation and re-reservation automatically.

The Core Difference: Geographic Scope

Pruvo checks for price drops in the same country and on the same platform where you originally booked. If you booked from the US on Booking.com, Pruvo monitors the US Booking.com price. This catches temporal price drops (the rate decreasing over time) but misses geographic price differences entirely.

Arbitrica searches 200 countries simultaneously. The same hotel room on the same platform often costs 20-60% less when viewed from a different country. This geographic gap is the largest source of potential savings, and Pruvo does not access it at all.

Setup and User Experience

Pruvo requires you to forward each booking confirmation email manually. If you forget to forward a booking, Pruvo does not track it. Arbitrica's Chrome extension detects bookings automatically. Every hotel reservation you make while the extension is active gets tracked without any action on your part.

When Pruvo finds a lower rate, you receive an email. You then need to go to the booking platform, cancel the old reservation, and rebook at the new rate yourself. Arbitrica handles the entire rebooking process with a single click after you review and approve the savings.

Which Tool Is Better?

Pruvo is a solid tool for travelers who want basic post-booking price monitoring with no cost. It catches temporal price drops reliably and has a simple setup process.

Arbitrica is the better choice for travelers who want maximum savings. The 200-country geographic search catches price differences that Pruvo cannot see, and the automatic rebooking eliminates the manual work of cancelling and re-reserving. For anyone booking hotels internationally, the geographic arbitrage alone typically produces 3-5x the savings of temporal tracking.
